What HPG Workspace is

A structured environment that combines project execution with recordkeeping that remains usable later. The aim is simple: reduce ambiguity about ownership, deadlines, and required artifacts.

Coordination across departments

Clear handoffs

When Finance, Development, Operations, Program, and General Counsel all depend on one another, the handoff is where delays often start. Workspace maps the sequence and the owner.

  • Defined step ownership
  • Required outputs attached to steps
  • Status visibility by role
Documentation with traceability

Records you can find

Decisions, agreements, budgets, and evidence can be stored with dates and context. If a question arises later, the timeline can be reconstructed.

  • Consistent naming and templates
  • Approvals captured with notes
  • Version history that stays readable
Dashboards for oversight

Routine visibility

Dashboards summarize activity without requiring multiple manual spreadsheets, while teams keep the operational detail in the work itself.

  • Portfolio summaries by initiative
  • Compliance coverage indicators
  • Reporting cadence and due dates

Dashboards and donut charts

Workspace dashboards can be arranged by department, initiative, or partner. Donut charts are used for quick coverage checks (what is complete, what is missing, and what needs review).

Example workflow: Fiscal Sponsorship intake to active project

This sequence illustrates how a partner could move from inquiry to an operating project with clear outputs at each step. Actual steps vary by agreement type and risk profile.

Step 1 Intake
Initial request and scope

Partner submits an intake form and core documents. Workspace routes the request to Development and Compliance and creates a trackable case with required artifacts.

Step 2 Due diligence
Verification and review

Review checklist collects questions, verification steps, and missing items. Decisions are recorded with dates and notes so the rationale remains available later.

Step 3 Agreement
Contract and onboarding

Legal packet is finalized, signature status is tracked, and onboarding tasks are assigned (folders, tool access, financial controls, reporting schedule).

Step 4 Readiness
Plan and setup

Budget, workplan, and measurable outputs are attached to the project record. Operations readiness is checked (logistics, training, procurement steps, and approvals).

Step 5 Run
Implementation and reporting

Recurring reporting cadence is set. Evidence and receipts are attached along the way, and dashboard views summarize progress for the appropriate leaders and reviewers.

FAQ

If your situation is unusual, contact the IT Support desk and include your role and initiative.

Is this a replacement for Google Drive, Slack, or Asana?

Usually no. Workspace is intended to coordinate work and maintain structured records while referencing the tools you already use. In practice, it can reduce back-and-forth by making ownership and required outputs explicit.

Who can see partner documents and compliance records?

Visibility depends on role assignment and project permissions. Compliance and legal items can be limited to the smallest responsible group, while project teams receive what they need to complete their assigned steps.

What should I include in an access request?

Your name, role, department or committee, initiative or project name (if applicable), and what you need to do in Workspace (tasks, review, uploads, dashboards).
